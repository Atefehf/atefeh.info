Front

Front Financial is an investing community powered by real investors and smart technology. It helps users to connect their brokerage accounts, get free portfolio scores and analyses, and get inspired by the community's points of view about investment.
Among the core features of the platform is portfolio optimization with the goal of educating the user and guiding them during their investment journey.

Optimizing the portfolio helps users to gain a better understanding of their portfolio's performance by adjusting the risk levels to see how they could perform better. You can use it to make better investment decisions if you need a hint.

The feature evolves over time based on feedback and analytics.

Current Situation

We designed the feature in webengage to be able to launch it quickly and test it because the idea hadn't been tested before. The design evolved in 3 phases

Ideation process

Problems with the first design

On one screen, there is too much information with different purposes. I must have dug deeper into the features and provided solutions based on the available technology and the users' needs so that we could guide the user through the journey in the least amount of confusion.

Proposed Solutions

First, I talked to our engineering team to gain a better understanding of what we can offer users. In addition to working with the engineering team, I ensured that compliance aligned with what we can offer users and how we can communicate it.

To discuss all of that with management, I created a few variations with a very tight deadline and limited resources.

Ideation

I have now defined the user's journey based on the information I collected. The following will be offered to the user:
  • What is the breakdown of their Front Score?

  • Portfolio Analysis

  • How can the portfolio be improved?

A. Merge the portfolio analysis with Front Score concept.

B. Sort the information into separate categories and keep the webengage.

Final Design

Following a few sketches, I was able to determine the information we can show the user on each tab. The user can then scan the page and click on the information that catches their attention the most.


Within the analysis page, I consolidated the Front Score information into a card and reduced the number of tabs to two.Our reasoning for removing the Front score tab is because we display it on the Portfolio page, where users naturally want to know what it is. Additionally, the breakdown does not need to be displayed on a separate tab every time.

Outcome

Despite changing the main CTA to Optimize my portfolio" on the portfolio screen, there is still only a 2% click on the button and only a very few users will get to this page.


I conducted some casual user testing inside the company to learn how people use the tabs and better understand their behavior. I then compiled a list of sections I thought could be improved.

Areas to improve

  • Use onboarding to be more clear and informative about the portfolio analysis.
  • Add a step by step in app guidance
  • Bring the feature to the portfolio page.
